Hey Data Scientist, I can't read your fontSize 10 text

Do you know what differentiate a Powerpoint prepared by data scientist and a Business Analyst? Certainly not a 2D t-SNE projection of your word-embeddings! It's the ugly style and extra-small font size we used in our chart!

Your insight is as best as your audience understand. Data Scientist spends a lot of time to drill insight from data, but not enough time to present their insight. Unfortunately, human perception is largely based on visual, a easy-to-read chart is much more likely to sell your idea with a custom matplotlib pyplot chart. There is nothing wrong with matplotlib, it is custom for a user sit in front of a monitor. When it comes to presentation, you really should make some adjustment for your audience. Luckily, it is easy to do with the following tips.

Make your charts fit in Powerpoint with Style Composition

This chart may be fine for normal use case, but if you want to put this chart into a PowerPoint, it is often too small for audience to read. Matplotlib actually allows you to compose two different style.

I created a presentation.mplstyle file as follow. I took some of the config from the Urban Theme mentioned here. https://towardsdatascience.com/consistently-beautiful-visualizations-with-altair-themes-c7f9f889602

axes.titlesize : 24
axes.labelsize : 20
lines.linewidth : 3
lines.markersize : 10
xtick.labelsize : 16
ytick.labelsize : 16
figure.figsize   : 12, 9

plt.style.use('presentation')
